About This Program

A patellar tracking and VMO strengthening protocol designed for quad tightness and patellar instability. Stretching is daily (10 min). Strength training is 4× per week (20 min). The 8-week mark is the key checkpoint — if symptoms haven't improved by then, imaging such as an MRI may be warranted.

How to Install as App

Open this page in Chrome on an Android device. Tap the three-dot menu (⋮) → "Add to Home screen" → "Add." The app will appear on the home screen and run fullscreen like a native app. All data is stored locally on the device.

Schedule

Every day: Stretching & mobility (couch stretch, Thomas test, foam roll, kneeling hip stretch).

4× per week (Mon/Wed/Fri/Sat recommended): Strength exercises (terminal knee extensions, Spanish squats, step-downs, single-leg wall sits, KB RDLs, lateral walks warm-up).

Before activity: Always do the couch stretch before any lower-body exercise or sport — 60 sec per side minimum.

When to Stop an Exercise

Discomfort and mild muscle burn are normal. Sharp pain, sudden instability, or anything that feels like the knee is shifting are not. Stop immediately if any of those occur. Rest a few days, continue gentle stretching, and resume once the acute pain passes.

Data & Privacy

All data is stored locally on the device using the browser's local storage. Nothing is transmitted externally. Clearing browser data will erase progress history.
